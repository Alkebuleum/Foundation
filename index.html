Why We Exist

Our Mission

The Alkebuleum Foundation exists to ensure the ecosystem grows with integrity — open, decentralised, and rooted in the communities it was built to serve. We do not control the chain. We serve it.

01

Research & Protocol

We fund independent research into protocol design, cryptography, consensus mechanisms, and security — ensuring Alkebuleum remains technically sound and future-proof.

02

Ecosystem Grants

We provide grants to builders, developers, and community organisers creating open-source public goods on or for the Alkebuleum ecosystem — no equity, no strings.

03

Education & Community

We support developer education, community events, and documentation efforts that lower the barrier to participation — especially across the African continent and diaspora.

How We Help

Foundation Programs

Three programmes through which the Foundation deploys resources into the ecosystem. All funded work is open-source and non-commercial.

Program One

Ecosystem Grants

Direct funding for open-source projects building on or for the Alkebuleum ecosystem. Proposals are submitted and voted on-chain via uGov.

How to Apply

01 Draft your proposal — problem, solution, roadmap, and budget
02 Submit on-chain at ugov.cc — requires a connected ALKE wallet
03 Community review period — 7 days open discussion and Q&A
04 On-chain vote — token-weighted, 5-day voting window
05 Funding released in milestones upon community verification
  • → Developer tooling & SDKs
  • → Security research & audits
  • → Community infrastructure & education
  • → Anti-corruption & governance tooling

Program Two

Research Initiative

Funding independent research aligned with the Alkebuleum Whitepaper — with AI and blockchain convergence as the ultimate horizon.

Active Research Tracks

◆ AI agent identity & on-chain reputation — bridging LLM behaviour with verifiable credentials
◆ Trustless AI execution rails — fast-finality AI computation checkpointed to L1
◆ Decentralised governance for African public institutions — accountability via smart contracts
◆ Sovereign digital identity — self-custody ID systems for underserved populations
◆ Zero-knowledge proofs for privacy-preserving civic applications
  • → All outputs published open-source
  • → University & independent researcher partnerships
  • → Whitepaper available at alkebuleum.org

Program Three

Builder Fellowship

A six-month programme for early-stage founders and developers building on Alkebuleum — funding, mentorship, and ecosystem access. No equity taken.

Fellowship Track

Month 1–2 Onboarding, scoping, and technical architecture review
Month 3–4 Active build phase with bi-weekly Foundation check-ins
Month 5–6 Launch preparation, ecosystem integration & demo day
  • → Direct funding — no equity taken
  • → Mentorship from Foundation & ecosystem leaders
  • → Access to uGov for community-backed proposals
  • → Demo day & investor introductions
